B. **Cooking Methods**
   - Boiling: The most common method for cooking pasta, boiling submerges the pasta in a large pot of salted water until it reaches desired tenderness.
   - Steaming: A healthier alternative to boiling, steaming involves placing the pasta on a steamer basket and cooking it over simmering water.
   - Pan-frying: This method involves cooking pasta directly in a hot pan with oil, resulting in crispy, flavorful noodles.
   - Baking: Pasta can also be baked, creating dishes like lasagna or stuffed shells that are layered and oven-baked to perfection.

C. **Sauce Interactions**
   - Tomato-based sauces pair well with long pasta types like spaghetti and fettuccine, while creamy sauces complement shorter, thicker varieties such as penne or fusilli.

D. **Ingredient Effects**
   - Cheese: Grated Parmesan, mozzarella, or ricotta can add richness and depth to pasta dishes.
   - Herbs: Basil, oregano, thyme, and parsley infuse pasta dishes with vibrant flavors.
   - Spices: Red pepper flakes, black pepper, and nutmeg contribute heat, complexity, and warmth to pasta recipes.
   - Garlic: A staple in many pasta dishes, garlic adds a pungent aroma and robust flavor.
   - Olive oil: Extra-virgin olive oil is often used as a base for sauces and contributes to the overall richness of the dish.
